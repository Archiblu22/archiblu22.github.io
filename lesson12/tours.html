COZUMEL
TOURS

Fun For Everyone!

There are so many fun things to do in Cozumel, Mexico! If you're looking for some extra fun adventures, see these other options of tours and activities you can participate in. A lot of them can be done in small or large groups. Sponsored by Tripadvisor.

Jeep Excursion

Mexico’s largest inhabited island, Cozumel boasts pristine Caribbean beaches and spectacular coral life. On this exclusive jungle and snorkeling tour, you’ll get a taste of the island’s ancient culture and natural beauty behind the wheel of your own private jeep—with time to savor local delicacies. Maximize your visit by covering all the island's must-see sites in one visit.

Food Tour

Pleasure your palate with meat-stuffed empanadas, succulent shrimp tacos, and sweet Mexican pastries on a culinary tour of Cozumel. Sample the exotic flavors of the idyllic island, noting the Mexican spice and Caribbean flair of the dishes due to Cozumel’s close proximity to Cuba. Immerse yourself in the culinary customs of the area as a knowledgeable guide takes you to five favorite local haunts to same fine Yucatan bites and beverages. Enjoy personalized attention and round-trip transportation on this intimate small group tour.

ATV Adventure

Make the most of limited time on this ATV adventure tour in Cozumel, a great choice for a shore excursion or for those who want to experience multiple island highlights in a single trip. Alongside your guide, drive your ATV through the jungle and down a series of trails to explore caves that make up the Yucatan’s extensive underwater river system and dive into a freshwater cenote. That leaves just enough time to ride through a coral cave and learn about Maya culture.

Submarine Expedition

Make the most of limited time and explore one of Cozumel’s most captivating coral reefs with a guide. Descend to depths of 100 feet (30 meters) and cruise along the ocean floor in the air-conditioned comfort of an Atlantis Submarine without getting wet. On this reef tour, you’ll learn about the marine ecosystem and see parts of the reef that were previously only accessible to scuba divers.
